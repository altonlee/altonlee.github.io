It's 2015. *Star Wars VII: The Force Awakens* had people of different generations in a chokehold, same sex marriage was legally recognized at a state and federal level, and people argued whether a dress was blue-black or white-gold. For me, this was the year that began my journey as a software developer. 

My first programming language was C#. I installed the Unity Engine and followed a 4-part Youtube series on creating a beginner 3D platformer where a cube parkours over walls and enemies to reach the flag. I was thrilled learning a new language and watching my cube character slide around for the first time, but the adrenaline quickly wore off when the tutorial referenced classes, pointers, headers, and worst of all, math. I was not properly introduced to variable types and the strict rules that came with class overloading and object properties, so I became frustrated and killed my game only one month into development. 

Learning JavaScript was a nice breath of fresh air. C# and its sister languages are quite strict; with compile complications, variable types and the inability to easily cast them, and the frustrating concept of pointers. Javascript on the other hand is very flexible and accessible for beginners. It is able to initialize basically anything as a ``var`` object type, and accessing an object's properties could not get any easier than bracket notation. There are also a plethora of APIs to plug-and-play anything you need Javascript to do! 

All software languages have a place and audience it appeals to. For me, I truly enjoy working with and learning Javascript, simply due to the versatility and accessibility Javascript allows its developers. In another world, had I built my cube parkour game in Javascript I may have something to show in this essay.
